about me

Engineer · Researcher · Educator

I'm a Research & Teaching Assistant at Zewail City of Science and Technology, Egypt, pursuing an MSc in Computer Science. My research sits at the intersection of autonomous vehicle systems, federated learning, and computer vision — specifically developing two-stream CNN architectures for steering angle prediction.

By day (and night), I also work as an Embedded Linux Software Engineer at Geneina, building low-level Linux systems, device drivers, and custom embedded distributions using Yocto and Buildroot. I have deep experience with ARM Cortex-M and AVR microcontrollers, FreeRTOS, and bare-metal systems programming.

Teaching

I've trained 500+ students across multiple institutions including Zewail City, ITI, IMT School, and Amit Learning. My courses cover embedded systems, IoT, C++ (including Qt), ARM Assembly, and Linux internals. I believe deeply in hands-on, lab-first teaching — all my courses ship with real hardware exercises.
