Active · MSc Thesis

Two-Stream CNN for Autonomous Vehicle Steering Angle Prediction

Implementing a dual-stream convolutional network combining a spatial RGB stream and a temporal optical flow stream for end-to-end steering angle prediction. The architecture processes both appearance and motion features in parallel before fusion.

Replicating and extending Zhang et al.'s federated learning automotive paper with full PyTorch implementation. Key challenges addressed include optical flow method selection (Farnebäck vs DIS vs TV-L1), FC layer dimensional alignment, and federated aggregation.

Key contributions

  • Full PyTorch implementation of two-stream CNN with fusion layers
  • Comparative study of optical flow methods for temporal stream
  • FedAvg and asynchronous FL variants for multi-client training
  • Benchmark suite measuring inference time, throughput, and network latency
PyTorch OpenCV Optical Flow FedAvg CNN Python

Federated Learning for Privacy-Preserving Autonomous Driving

Designing and evaluating federated learning infrastructure for autonomous driving models. Implemented FederatedServer and FederatedClient classes supporting both synchronous FedAvg and asynchronous aggregation strategies.

Focus areas include communication efficiency (gradient compression, sparse updates), convergence analysis across heterogeneous data distributions, and privacy guarantees through differential privacy mechanisms.
